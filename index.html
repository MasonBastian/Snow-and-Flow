Our Mission

Snow & Flow is dedicated to snowboarding culture — sharing stories, tips, history, and snowy vibes from the mountains to the web.

Tips for Beginners

  • Take a lesson. Starting with a certified instructor can save you from developing bad habits early on.
  • Learn how to fall. Try to fall on your forearms or butt — not your wrists!
  • Wear proper gear. A helmet, wrist guards, and waterproof layers can make all the difference.
  • Stay on beginner trails. Stick to green runs until you’ve got your balance and turns dialed in.
  • Look where you want to go. Your body follows your eyes — don’t stare at the ground or trees.
  • Be patient with yourself. Snowboarding takes time — but it’s worth it!

The Origins of Snowboarding

Snowboarding didn’t fall from the sky — it tore down the slopes of history, a blend of imagination, rebellion and a ton of powder.

It began in 1965, when a Michigan man named Sherman Poppen strapped together two skis to create a toy for his daughter. He named it the “Snurfer.” Kids loved it — and it started a whole new winter trend.

By the 70s and 80s, pioneers like Jake Burton Carpenter and Tom Sims refined the design with edges and bindings. Ski resorts weren’t stoked at first, but the sport powered through.

Snowboarding became an Olympic sport in 1998 — and now it’s a lifestyle embraced across the world.

10 Fun Facts About Snowboarding

  1. The first snowboard was a toy. Sherman Poppen built the “Snurfer” in 1965.
  2. Snowboarding was once banned. Resorts in the 80s thought it was too rebellious.
  3. It became an Olympic sport in 1998. In Nagano, Japan.
  4. Shaun White won X Games medals at 13. A true prodigy.
  5. It has skateboarding roots. Many tricks are straight out of skate culture.
  6. Fastest speed on a snowboard: 125 mph. Wild.
  7. SSX Tricky is a cult-favorite video game. If you know, you know.
  8. Burton was founded in 1977. It helped shape the modern snowboard.
  9. Terrain parks revolutionized riding. Rails, jumps, and creativity unlocked.
  10. Snowboarding is about style. It’s self-expression on snow.
